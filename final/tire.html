Step 1: Find A Safe Location

As soon as you realize you have a flat tire, do not abruptly brake or turn. Slow down gradually and scan for a straight stretch of road with a wide shoulder. Pull over to a safe, flat location away from traffic. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers. Use wheel wedges to prevent your car from rolling, and set your parking brake.

Step 3: Loosen the Lug Nuts

Use the lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts on the flat tire by turning them counterclockwise. Do not remove them completely yet.

Step 4: Jack Up The Car

Place the jack under the vehicle at the jack point closest to the flat tire. Raise the car with the jack until the flat tire is about six inches off the ground.

Step 5: Replace The Tire

Remove the lug nuts and flat tire. Mount the spare tire onto the lug bolts, hand-tighten the lug nuts, and lower the car. Tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern.

Step 6: Store Flat Tire and Tools

Place the flat tire and tools back in your vehicle. Check the pressure of the spare tire and drive carefully. Get your flat tire repaired or replaced as soon as possible.
